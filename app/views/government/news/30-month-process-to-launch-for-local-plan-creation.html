30-month process to launch for creating local plans

New approach focuses on reducing the time it takes to create and update plans

From:
Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities
Published
18 December 2023

We have set out a new approach for local plan making, which revolves around a 30-month process from starting to launching a local plan.

The new process has been created after extensive consultation and feedback from everyone involved in the process. It reflects the need of communities to have more structured involvement in the creation of a local plan, and for that plan to be published sooner. 

The new 30-month process will come into effect from November 2024.

We have also published extensive information to support this new policy. It collates the different stages of this new process, giving plan makers one place to learn more and get support.

What you need to know

New information on the process includes:

  • information on how plan-making can be done in 30 months

  • resources and templates to help standardise parts of the process

  • explanation of ‘gateway’ checkpoints for validating plans

Where to learn more

You can read a summary of the new timeline in the 30-month process explained.

It sets out how the process breaks down, and includes what plan makers are expected to do at each stage.
