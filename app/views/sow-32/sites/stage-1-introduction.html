Background

Selecting sites allows you to identify land that could be used for housing or employment property building, as well as other land uses.

It’s an essential part of preparing your local plan and delivering a spatial strategy. Comprehensive site selection helps you deliver on your plan-making needs and objectives.

The site process involves:

  • identifying sites in your area for development
  • assessing sites to check if they are right for your development needs
  • creating a list of sites you can use for development
  • selecting which sites you will use to add into your local plan

How to use this information

This supporting guidance gives local plan makers a more consistent approach to site selection.

It focuses on:

  • a framework and supporting methodologies for identifying sites
  • what data to collect during the call for sites process
  • how to analyse your identified sites
  • an approach to selecting the most appropriate sites
  • creating a narrative on why you selected these sites

All this is designed to help show you’ve followed a sound approach to site selection, and therefore increase the likelihood you are successful at examination.

What this information covers

No two local authorities are the same, or face the same challenges. Therefore this guidance does not replace local planning judgement. It is flexible enough to account for local circumstances, and we encourage you to adapt it to your needs.

Where you deviate from this guidance during your plan-making process, it’s important to document when and why. This will help you make your case during evaluation.

This guidance is only for housing or employment property site selection.
