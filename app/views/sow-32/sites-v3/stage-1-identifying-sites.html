Aim of this section

  • understand how to reach all potential sites available in your local area
  • know the size and type of sites you need
  • know where to look for these sites

Finding all potential sites

Start by considering any existing sites that could be improved, intensified or changed. You should not consider any constraints at this point.

You should look at sites in:

  • local or neighbourhood plans
  • the brownfield land register
  • planning applications (permissions and refusals)
  • pre-application submissions (in agreement with developers)
  • current or previous land availability assessments
  • land owned by the local authority and other public bodies in consultation
  • new or previous capacity or intensification assessment
  • vacant and derelict land and buildings
  • land in alternative use that may be suitable for re-development, such as commercial buildings or car parks
  • opportunities to intensify uses or develop areas
  • opportunities to extend existing settlements

For discovering new land opportunities, you should run a call for sites.

Size and type of sites you need

This framework covers sites for housing and employment (including mixed use sites). If you wish to consider alternative types than those listed, make sure to document your reasoning ahead of assessment.

You should identify different size sites from your area. This includes small-scale sites to those with opportunities for larger-scale development.

You should apply a sites threshold that suits your needs. This can change as well, so you do not have to discount sites at an early stage to meet an unsuitable threshold later on.

Make sure you consider all sites that can deliver five or more homes.

Example housing sites include:

  • market housing for sale or rent (including build to rent and co-living)
  • affordable housing
  • specialist elderly housing
  • self-build or custom-build housing
  • student accomodation

The threshold for employment sites should be of at least 0.25 hectares (or 500 square metres of floor space).

Example employment sites include:

  • offices, leisure and retail
  • research and development and knowledge
  • industrial buildings
  • logistics
  • data centres
  • employment-led mixed use

The exact type of sites will depend on whatâ€™s available to you locally.
