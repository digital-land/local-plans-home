The site selection process is a way to identify land you can use to build housing or employment property, as well as other land uses.

The process involves: 

  • identifying sites in your area for development
  • testing sites to check if they are right for your development needs
  • creating a list of sites you can use for development
  • choosing which sites you will use to add into your local plan

The site selection process could also be informed by or help feed into your spatial strategy. This means your sites will align with your local plan and strategy for the area.

To help local authorities select sites, we have created a framework you should follow. This sets out how you can identify, assess and allocate sites for housing and employment development.

We have created this framework and guidance to help standardise the process for site selection. This process should increase the likelihood of success at examination by showing you’ve created a sound plan. Where there is local context meaning you cannot follow parts of this process, you should clearly document and justify this. 

What is not covered in this framework

This framework is designed for assessing sites for housing and employment development only. To assess any other sites, you’ll need to use a different process, or adapt this one.
